If you're making content people love, there are three ways to get paid for it on Whamr. This is how each one works.

First the headline: Whamr takes 20% of everything you earn. You keep 80%. That ratio doesn't change based on how much you make, what tier you're on, or how long you've been around. One split, every time.

## 1. Premium content (one-off unlocks)

The simplest way to earn. You mark any post as Premium. People see a blurred preview and a price. They tap to unlock. Money lands in your account.

**How it works:**

- You set the price, between $0.99 and $9.99
- A blur overlay goes on the post in public feeds with an "Unlock for $X" button
- When someone pays, Stripe handles the transaction and instantly credits your account
- They get permanent access to that post (not a time-limited rental)

**What works as Premium content:**

Typically: behind-the-scenes stuff, extended cuts, higher-resolution versions, unreleased material, bonus packs, the "part 2" of a series. Content that has clear standalone value beyond your free posts.

What doesn't usually work: a random meme you'd normally post for free, just with a price tag on it. People recognise the pattern and don't pay.

**What to expect:**

Premium content tends to convert best when you build a free audience first. A follower who's enjoyed 20 free posts is much more likely to pay for a premium one than a stranger who just found your profile. Think of it as a funnel: free posts earn trust, premium posts monetise that trust.

## 2. Creator subscriptions (monthly recurring)

Monthly subscriptions turn one-off unlocks into recurring income. Subscribers pay you every month and get access to all your premium content for as long as they're subscribed.

**How it works:**

- You set a monthly price, between $1.99 and $19.99
- Subscribers pay monthly through Stripe
- They get access to every premium post you've made and will make, as long as their subscription is active
- Cancellations don't affect past access (they keep what they paid for in the current billing period)

**Why this matters:**

Recurring revenue is predictable revenue. If you have 100 subscribers at $5/month, you know you have $500 coming in next month. That kind of baseline lets you plan, invest in better equipment, or spend more time making content.

The math works out differently for subscriptions versus one-offs:

- 100 one-off unlocks at $4.99 = $499 once
- 100 subscribers at $4.99/month = $499/month, or $5,988/year

The second one doesn't happen overnight, but it compounds.

**What works as subscription content:**

Consistency. Subscribers expect a regular flow of new premium content each month. If you subscribe to someone and they drop one post in three months, you unsubscribe. A rhythm of one or two premium posts per week works well for most creators.

## 3. Brand marketplace (coming v3.0)

The third revenue stream is brand deals, facilitated through a marketplace inside Whamr. This one is rolling out in v3.0, but here's how it'll work.

**The brand side:**

Brands post a brief describing what they want: the content format, the target audience, the budget range, and the deadline. Briefs are reviewed by the Whamr team before they're published to creators.

**The creator side:**

Creators browse available briefs and submit proposals. A proposal includes your approach, your price, and your timeline. The brand reviews proposals and picks one.

**The flow once matched:**

- Brand payment goes into escrow
- Creator produces the content
- Brand reviews and approves
- Escrow releases to the creator (minus the platform fee)
- Content goes live with a clear "Sponsored" badge

The platform fee for brand deals is 15% total, split as 5% from the brand and 10% from the creator. So the creator's effective take-home is still around 90% of their listed price.

**What to expect:**

Brand deals tend to work best for creators who have a distinct voice or a clear niche. Brands hire specific aesthetics, not generic meme accounts. Build something recognisable first. The briefs will find you.

## Payouts, explained

Regardless of which revenue stream you're earning from, the payout mechanics are the same.

**Minimum threshold:** $25. You have to earn at least this much before you can request a payout. Below $25, your earnings keep accumulating.

**Payout methods:** Stripe Connect (direct to your bank) or PayPal. You pick in settings. Stripe Connect is usually faster and has better currency conversion rates if you're outside the US.

**Payout frequency:** Monthly is standard. The payout job runs on the 1st of each month for all earnings accrued the previous month. You can also manually request a payout anytime you're above the threshold.

**Currencies:** USD, EUR, GBP, BRL, INR (with more coming as internationalisation expands). Stripe handles the conversion automatically.

**Tax:** You're responsible for your own taxes. Whamr issues a 1099 form (US) or equivalent end-of-year summary if you've earned over the reporting threshold in your region. Talk to a tax professional — we can't advise here.

## The creator dashboard

Everything is visible in one place: your total lifetime earnings, this month's earnings (updated daily), the breakdown between premium purchases, subscriptions, and brand deals, your top 5 earning posts, and an earnings graph over 7, 30, and 90 days.

There's also a payout history showing every withdrawal, the amount, the date, and the method. Clean, auditable, tax-ready.

## Getting started

Before any of this kicks in, you need to apply for creator status. The application is straightforward:

1. Make sure your Whamr account has at least 3 posts
2. Link your other social accounts (optional but helps)
3. Submit your best 3 pieces of content as your application portfolio
4. Wait 72 hours for review

If approved, you connect your Stripe or PayPal account in settings, and monetisation is enabled on your profile. You can switch a post to Premium the same day.

That's the whole thing. Three revenue streams, one clear split, one dashboard. If you make content that travels, you can earn from it here.
